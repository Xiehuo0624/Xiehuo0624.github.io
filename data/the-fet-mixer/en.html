The FET Mixer is a 12-input 4-output spatial mixer. Its primary inspiration comes from Christina Kubisch's Cloud and The Mixer Project, and Cathy Van Eck's book Microphones and loudspeakers. In this mixer, 12 audio inputs feed into 12 PCB antennas, converting analog audio signals into electromagnetic waves, which are then converted back to analog audio signals by inductors and amplifiers. Thus, 12 invisible sound sources exist on the instrument's panel, waiting to be picked up by the audience moving sensors across them.

In this work, I attempt to explore the possibility of mixing as the sole method of musical creation, and how an instrument can serve as a bridge between the performer's physical gestures and sound. The former is inspired by The Mixer Project, the latter by Cathy Van Eck's book Microphones and loudspeakers.

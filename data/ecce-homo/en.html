The Roman governor Pontius Pilate pointed to Jesus — wearing the crown of thorns, draped in a purple robe, his hands bound — and called out to the people of Jerusalem: "Ecce homo." Since then, interpretations and anecdotes surrounding depictions of the crowned Christ have never ceased; what shifts endlessly are the perspectives and figures, while what remains is the myriad faces of human good and evil.

At this point, he very reluctantly looked at the face of the dead man. It was as it had been in life; no sign of the promised redemption was to be detected; what all the others had found in the machine the officer had not found; the lips were firmly pressed together, the eyes were open, with the same expression as in life, the look was calm and convinced, through the forehead went the point of the great iron spike.

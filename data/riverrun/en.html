This project is an interactive sound installation based on postmodern literary text and hardware interaction. The work uses the original The FET Mixer, combined with a multi-track audiobook of James Joyce's Finnegans Wake, transforming it into a sonic space where the audience can mix, experience, and reinterpret the work on their own terms.

Finnegans Wake is renowned for its intricate multilingual wordplay and cyclical narrative structure — the text itself possesses an inherently "multi-track" quality. This installation creates an isomorphic mapping between the electromagnetic waves at the physical level and the polysemy at the textual level. By tracing the different etymological roots of individual words in the original text, recording each as a separate audiobook track, and placing them across The FET Mixer's panel for the audience to mix, the work breaks away from traditional linear reading or passive listening. In doing so, it presents the possibilities of postmodern literature and explores the deep connections between language, sound, instrument hardware, and the audience's bodily interaction.

The raw sonic material consists of pre-recorded multi-track audiobook readings of Finnegans Wake. The text processing references Dai Congrong's Chinese translation, deconstructing and distributing the different interpretations of the book's portmanteau words. For example, the opening "riverrun" can be deconstructed as:
● river run
● reverend
● Erinnerung [German] (memory)
Following this deconstructive approach, the first passage was recorded in five versions each, using different pronunciation styles (alternating Chinese and English), forming multiple independent sound tracks. Key track texts are as follows:
